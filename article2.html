Website vs. Facebook Page: What’s Better for Your Local Business?

For small businesses across Lincolnshire and beyond, having a strong online presence is key to attracting new customers. But many local business owners ask the same question — do I need a website, or is a Facebook page enough? While both have their place, they serve different purposes. Here's a clear breakdown to help you decide what’s best for your business.

Why Being Online Matters

Whether you're a builder, barber, or bakery, customers are looking you up online. If all they find is a basic Facebook page — or worse, nothing at all — they might go with a competitor who looks more professional. A well-designed website shows you take your business seriously.

Why a Website Is Worth It

A proper website is more than just an online brochure. It gives you control over your branding, makes you look credible, and helps you show up on Google when people search for your services. With your own domain, you can display your services, pricing, testimonials, FAQs, and much more — all in one place.

  • Rank higher in search engines (SEO)
  • Build trust with a clean, professional look
  • Customise everything to fit your brand
  • Convert visitors into enquiries or bookings

When Facebook Pages Work Best

A Facebook page is a great way to post updates, get reviews, and connect with your local audience. It’s quick to set up and free to use — perfect for staying active and sharing photos or promotions. But it does have limitations.

  • Limited design and layout options
  • Not great for Google visibility
  • Posts can get buried in the newsfeed
  • You're dependent on Facebook’s rules and algorithm

The Best Option: Use Both Together

Ideally, your website should be your main hub — the place where all your key info lives. Your Facebook page can then support that by helping you connect with your audience and drive traffic to your site. Think of it like this: your website shows who you are, your Facebook page shows what you’re up to.

Final Thoughts

In short: if you're serious about growing your local business, don’t rely on Facebook alone. A professional website helps you stand out, rank in Google, and convert visitors into customers. A Facebook page is a bonus — a way to stay active and social. Together, they make a powerful combo for your online presence.
